How we work

From the first
call to the
delivered archive.

Every project moves through the same eight phases, regardless of tier. The phase lengths change, the principles do not. Most campaigns take six weeks; the shortest editorial shipped in eleven days; the longest marque cycle ran for ten months.

Discovery

Twenty-minute first call with the producer. Brief, references, working dates, audience, where the work lands. Free, always.

Scope

Within 72 hours: a fixed scope, fixed rate, and proposed working window. No estimate creep.

Pre-production

Casting, location lock, props, talent, light maps. The producer holds every contract, every release.

Kickoff

One-hour creative call with the lead and the brand. Agree the visual rules, agree the deliverables.

Shoot

1 to 5 days. Tethered to a calibrated monitor. Daily live galleries for the brand-side approver.

Selects

Within 24 hours of wrap, a colour-managed gallery of all keepers — ranked, captioned, ready to choose.

Retouch

5 to 10 business days in-house. Two rounds of revisions standard, more on marque.

Delivery

Master TIFF, sized JPG, print TIFF, contact sheets, signed model releases — all in one delivery package.

Frequent questions

The questions
brands ask first.

These come up on roughly every third call. If your question is not here, send the producer a note — the address is on the contact page.

How far ahead do you book?

Three to five months for Campaign and Marque, four to six weeks for Editorial. We block specific weeks once a deposit is in.

Do you travel?

Yes — we work globally and have shot in 14 countries since 2014. Travel is quoted on top of the rate at cost; we never markup flights or accommodation.

What about usage rights?

Always negotiated upfront, never bolted on later. We licence by use, region, and duration — and we are happy to draft a buyout if the project genuinely needs one.

Can we book just the room?

Yes. Both rooms are available to outside crews for a limited number of weeks per year. The Brooklyn rate card is on request — ask Felix.

Do you do AI or generative imagery?

No. We make pictures. We will gladly retouch, composite, and grade — but the image we ship is the image that came out of the camera.

What if we hate the first round?

Every campaign tier includes two rounds of revisions. If the second round still misses, we re-shoot at studio cost — this has happened twice in eleven years, and we shipped both.
